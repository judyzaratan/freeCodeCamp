Alan Turing

“Sometimes it is the people no one can imagine anything of who do the things no one can imagine.” Alan Turing

Alan Turing was a British cryptanalyst that played a major part in deciphering the Nazi Engima machine, a tool utilized by German forces in encoding messages during World War II. Sadly, later in his life, he was convicted for being a homosexual. As a sentence for his conviction, he chose to be chemically castrated instead of prison. Turing died a couple of years after from cyanide poisoning. In 2013, he was posthumously pardoned by Queen Elizabeth II.
